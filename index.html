What is Low-Ops?

Low-Ops is an Internal Developer Platform (IDP) designed specifically for private cloud and on-premise deployments. It transforms your existing infrastructure into a secure, powerful platform for Mendix app development and deployment, all within your controlled environment.

For Platform Owners

Install Low-Ops on your existing infrastructure and start deploying Mendix apps in minutes. No need to spend years building a custom private cloud platform. We have reinvented the wheel (Platform as a Service) so that you don't have to.

  1. Turnkey solution to setup a private cloud platform for Mendix apps.
  2. Security and compliance across the whole platform
  3. Low platform maintenance with self-healing and self-cleanup capabilities

For Developers

Low-Ops provides a modern and user-friendly developers portal that empowers developers to quickly and easily deploy Mendix apps. With end-to-end lifecycle management and self-service capabilities, developers can focus on building innovative solutions without the need for extensive coordination and communication with other teams.

  1. Modern and friendly developers portal
  2. End-to-end lifecycle management of apps
  3. Self-service capabilities for optimal productivity

Speed and Control

  • 01 Private cloud platform setup takes months to start and years to mature.

    Low-Ops effectively addresses the challenge of lengthy private cloud platform setup and maturation by offering a seamless installation process that takes only 10 minutes. With Low-Ops, organizations can quickly and effortlessly install the platform on their existing infrastructure, bypassing the need for extensive setup procedures that typically take months to complete. Low-Ops doesn't compromise on the maturity of the platform. This eliminates potential bottlenecks and delays, enabling faster and more efficient application delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who is Low-Ops for?

    Low-Ops is meant for organizations that need flexibility and control over their Mendix applications and infrastructure. Some industries that can benefit from Low-Ops are: financial services, government, healthcare, education, and manufacturing.

  • What are system requirements?

    1. Kubernetes or Openshift Cluster
    2. AWS, GCP, Azure, other cloud provider or your own infrastructure
    3. Wildcard SSL certificate and DNS subdomain
    4. Mendix On-premise License

  • How Long Does it take to setup a private cloud with Low-ops?

    2 Hours when all system requirements are met. It also depends on your internet speed and agility of your organization.

  • Is this a service or a product?

    Low-Ops Platform is a product you can install on your own infrastructure. Once installed you can offer it as a Service to your developers. This type of platform is also known as platform as a service (PaaS).

  • How do I keep my platform secure and up to date?

    We take security and compliance seriously. We provide a secure and compliant platform that is designed to meet the highest security standards. We rigorously test upgrade paths of Low-Ops platform so that you can upgrade at ease.
